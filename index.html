Context

  • Both Greek epics attributed to Homer.
    • Believed to have been a blind bard from Ionia, now in Turkey.

Odysseus Stranded

  • Pronunciation of Ὠγυγίη: Ogygia
  • The Odyssey begins with Odysseus stranded on an island, Ὠγυγίη, with the nymph Calypso (Καλυψω).
  • He cannot leave as he has angered Poseidon.
  • Odysseus, of course, wishes to return to his family in Ithaca.

Ithaca

  • Athena, given the suffering Odysseus has endured, persuades Zeus to allow him to return home while Poseidon is absent from Mount Olympus.
  • She informs Telemachus, disguised as a chieftain, that Odysseus is alive
  • Telemachus sets off to find him with a newly assembled crew.

Assembly

  • Before he leaves, he makes an attempt to remove the suitors by calling an assembly of citizens.
    • However, the suitors ignore this, mocking Telemachus.

Telemachus's Search

  • He sails to Pylos, home to King Nestor.
    • Who refers him to Menelaus, as he says…

9 Years Ago

  • Diomedes also went with them.
  • After the Trojan War, Agamemnon and Menelaus (brothers) were arguing, which divided the returning forces.
  • Menelaus, along with Odysseus, took their armies and left.
  • Odysseus turned back, however, to confront Agamemnon.

Menelaus

  • Telemachus meets with Menelaus and Helen, his wife, in Sparta.
  • Menelaus tells, during his return to Sparta by way of Egypt, he encountered the sea-god Proteus.
  • And that Proteus had told him of Odysseus being stranded with Calypso.
  • Also, Telemachus learns that Agamemnon had been murdered returning from Troy by his wife and her lover.

Back In Ithaca

  • Suitors have learned of Telemachus's departure.
  • They plan to ambush his ship and kill him upon his return.

Odysseus Freed

  • Hermes delivers this message.
  • Odysseus spurns off Calypso's advances.
  • On Zeus's orders, persuaded by Athena, Calypso lets Odysseus go.
  • Odysseus constructs a raft and is given clothing, food and drink by Calypso who, by then, had fallen deeply in love with him.
  • Poseidon learns of Odysseus's escape and destroys his raft via storm.
  • However, helped by a veil given to him by the sea nymph Ino, he swims to Phaeacia (Scheria).

Phaeacia

  • Encouraged by Athena, the princess Nausicaa, finds Odysseus washed up.
  • She guides him to her parents, Arete and Alcinous, Queen & King of Scheria.
  • Odysseus tells them of his journey.

Odysseus's Journey

Journey Map

Raiding of Troy

  • Greeks have just raided Troy, Odysseus warns others to turn back while they are ahead.
  • They continue fighting and soon are overrun.
  • Odysseus and some others take the ships to escape but Zeus is angry at the Greeks for their poor sportsmanship and sends a storm to blow them off course.
  • Odysseus and his crew are swept far out to sea.

Lotus Eaters

  • First they wash up at the land of the lotus-eaters, anyone who eats a lotus wishes to never leave the island.
  • Most of the crew eat lotus and Odysseus has to force them back to the ship.

Island of The Cyclopes

  • They set out again, now washing up on the island of the cyclopes.
  • Odysseus and his crew enter Polythemus’s cave while he is out, expecting the cyclopse’s "Guest-friendship" (ξενία) upon his return.
  • However, when Polythemus returns, he traps them in and precedes to eat two of the crew.

Cunning Odysseus

  • Polythemus falls asleep and the following day Odysseus introduces himself as "noman", offering him undiluted wine.
  • They get him drunk and Odyessus pokes out the Cyclop’s eye with a hot wooden stake.
  • The other cylclopes offer no aid as all there hear is cries of "no man is hurting me", etc.

Blunder

  • But, as he leaves, Odysseus wishes for the Prometheus to know his name and tells him.
  • Polythemus, son of Poseidon, then cries out Odysseus’s name, from then on Poseidon aims to hinder Odysseus’s journey.

Aeolian Island & Laestrygonian

  • The crew arrive at the Aeolian island, the king, Aeolus, there gives them a pouch full of wind.
  • The crew open it near Ithaca, almost back home, bringing them back to Aeolian.
  • They Continue to Laestrygonian, populated by cannibal giants who eat the majority of Odysseus’s crew.

Circe

  • Go to Aeaea, where the daughter of Helios lives.
  • Circe turns Odysseus’s crew into pigs.
  • Hermes protects Odyesseus from Circe’s magic.
  • Odysseus threatens Circe and forces her to turn his crew back.
  • Circe affirms Tiresias is the one who knows how Odysseys can get home.
  • However, Tiresias is dead so Odysseus sails down to Hades.
  • Tiresias says that they may make it back if they do not harm any of the cattle of Helios on Thrinacia.

Back to Circe

  • Goes back to Aeaea where Circe plans out the route.
  • The route back to Ithaca is conveniently full of monsters, adding much drama and death to the rest of the story.

Journey Map

Encounters

  • First encounter the sirens, entrancing passing sailors with their music, luring them to their deaths.
  • Crew block their ears with earwax. However, Odysseus wanting to hear the siren’s music orders his crew to tie him to the mast of the ship as they pass so he does not flee to the sirens.
  • Odysseus and his crew then pass between the six headed monster - Scylla (who claims eight of the crew’s lives) and Charybdis - a whirlpool.

Thrinacia

  • Remember, this defies what Tiresias says in the underworld.
  • The crew land on the island Thrinacia where food begins to run out
  • Eurylochus, one of the crew, sacrifices one of the cows to the gods.
  • In punishemnet, as they leave, Zeus stikes the ship with lightning, killing all crew members, leaving Odyssyes alone.
  • Odysseus washes up on Calypso’s island, where he remains for seven years.

Return To Present

  • Back to Odysseus in Phaeacia, the King gives Odysseus a ship and crew and Odysseus sails to Ithaca.

  • Athena persuades the rest of Ithaca not to take revenage on Odyesseus for the slaughter.
